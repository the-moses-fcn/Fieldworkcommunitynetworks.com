This network belongs
to the community.
That means you.

Fieldwork is in active conversations across four categories — community members, institutional partners, capital sources, and peer networks. We are not collecting interest forms. We are having real conversations about real buildout. If one of these describes you, reach out.

01

Community members

If you live in the Salmon Creek watershed, Redway, Garberville, Miranda, Phillipsville, or anywhere along the southern 101 corridor — we want to know you exist and what you need. Early sign-ups inform coverage priority. Your address is useful to us right now, before the first tower goes up.

02

Fire departments & public institutions

If you represent a fire department, county agency, school, clinic, or public safety facility in or near the corridor — this network is built around you. The anchor institution model means the infrastructure deploys to your site first, costs you nothing, and generates revenue for your community. Let's talk about your response area.

03

Investors & capital partners

Phase 2 compute infrastructure is financed separately from the USDA grant by venture capital. If you are a family office, impact fund, or individual investor with interest in rural infrastructure, community-owned networks, or distributed edge compute — the financial model is built and the opportunity is real.

04

Community network builders

If you are building or planning a similar network in another rural community — we are documenting everything. The manufacturer relationships, the grant application framework, the LLC structure, the fire department anchor model. We believe the replication of this model is the point. Talk to us before you start.
