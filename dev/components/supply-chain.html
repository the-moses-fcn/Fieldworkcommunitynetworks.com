Hardware Supply Chain

We buy from the people
who build it.

Most rural broadband projects buy equipment off a shelf, mark it up, and call it infrastructure. Fieldwork Community Networks is building direct dealer relationships with every manufacturer in our supply chain — so the margin that typically goes to a middleman stays in the community that owns this network.

The Salmon Creek watershed build requires hardware from ten manufacturers across five categories: fixed wireless, private LTE, LoRaWAN sensors, solar and battery storage, and environmental cameras. At retail that hardware totals over $600,000 across the fleet. At dealer and distributor pricing, that number is meaningfully lower — and the difference stays inside Fieldwork.

More than the margin, dealer relationships give us something the grant application can document: letters of supply commitment from manufacturers confirming that Fieldwork has authorized access to their product lines at commercial pricing. That's procurement infrastructure, not just a purchase order. It demonstrates to USDA reviewers that this network is being built by people who have done the work to stand it up properly.

Every dollar we save on procurement is a dollar that stays in Southern Humboldt. Dealer pricing isn't a business detail — it's a community benefit.

Why This Matters

01

Community Margin

Dealer pricing on $700K+ in hardware generates tens of thousands in margin that stays inside the LLC — directly funding operations, maintenance, and expansion rather than leaving the watershed.

02

Grant Credibility

Manufacturer commitment letters are documented leveraged resources. They strengthen the USDA application by proving real procurement relationships, not a shopping list assembled the week the application was due.

03

Replication Ready

Once Fieldwork holds dealer accounts, every subsequent node — across the 101 corridor and beyond — buys at the same wholesale price. The first node pays for the relationship. Every node after benefits from it.

04

Supply Chain Control

Direct relationships mean direct communication when supply chains tighten. A community-owned network can't afford to wait for a reseller to get restocked. We talk to the manufacturer directly.
