Why We Exist

The fiber runs
right past us.
No one built the last mile.

The US 101 corridor through Southern Humboldt passes directly alongside one of the highest-capacity fiber backbones in the state. Most people — including most policymakers — don't know it's there. It has been there for decades.

Commercial carriers have had full access to that backbone for years. They have not built last-mile service into Salmon Creek, Redway, Miranda, Phillipsville, or Garberville. They have not done so because the economics don't work for a profit-driven provider serving a dispersed rural population in rugged terrain. That calculus will never change for a commercial carrier.

It changes for a community institution. Fieldwork Community Networks exists because community capital — not commercial capital — can solve this problem. We are building something a carrier structurally cannot build: locally owned, solar powered, accountable to the people it serves, and financially structured to sustain itself without extracting from its community.
