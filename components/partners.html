Built alongside the

Fieldwork Community Networks is not a company that arrived in Southern Humboldt with a product to sell. It is built alongside the fire departments, community media, and communications infrastructure that this watershed has relied on for generations.

Founding Anchor Institution

Salmon Creek Volunteer Fire Department

Primary deployment site and founding anchor institution. Dedicated 100 Mbps broadband, CBRS private LTE across the response area, autonomous drone response capability, and founding membership in the Emergency AI Cooperative — at no cost to the department.

Anchor Institution Partner

Redway Fire Protection District

Anchor institution partner serving the Redway community. Dedicated broadband and private LTE for the response area, distribution node infrastructure at no cost to the district.

Gateway Node Partner

Garberville Fire Protection District

Host of the network's primary gateway node — the fiber interconnect point where the 101 backbone enters the community network. Solar canopy installation and carrier co-location revenue sharing.

Tower Co-Location Partner

KMUD Redwood Community Radio

35 years of community-owned communications infrastructure. Tower co-location partner providing the elevation and institutional credibility that no commercial site can replicate. We are building the next layer of the same story.

Primary Ridge Node

Last Mile Digital / Pratt Mountain

Tower co-location at 3,880 ft elevation — the irreplaceable ridge node providing line-of-sight microwave backhaul and point-to-multipoint distribution across the watershed. Active tower rental negotiation underway.

Fraternal Partner

Humboldt Lodge No. 79, F.&A.M.

The oldest fraternal organization in Humboldt County, supporting a brother doing meaningful work for the communities of Southern Humboldt.
