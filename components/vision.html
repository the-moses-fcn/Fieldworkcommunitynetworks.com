The Replication Model

Salmon Creek is
the proof of concept.
101 is the corridor.

Every rural community along the US 101 corridor with ridgeline access, proximity to the MMBI fiber backbone, a fire department willing to anchor, and a population that has been waiting for someone to do this — is a candidate for this model. The proof of concept in Salmon Creek becomes the template. The grant becomes the precedent. The LLC structure becomes the franchise.

Fieldwork Community Networks is not building one network. It is building the first node in a replicable network of community-owned rural broadband, emergency communications, and distributed edge infrastructure across the high fire hazard severity zones of Northern California — a string of solar-powered micro-infrastructure sites from the Salmon Creek watershed northward through the 101 corridor, each anchored by a fire department, each generating value for the community that hosts it.

Phase 2 deploys commercial compute infrastructure at the primary Garberville site — financed separately by venture capital, not grant funds. AI inference, edge processing, and distributed compute generate a revenue stream that further stabilizes the network and funds corridor expansion without returning to federal grants.

Beginning in Year 3, retained earnings from the established Salmon Creek subscriber base self-fund one additional node deployment every eighteen months — no new grant required. There are twenty to thirty viable locations in Humboldt County alone where the same formula holds. Southern Humboldt is the first. It is not the only.

What the proof of concept establishes

The Salmon Creek build doesn't just deliver broadband — it documents a replicable system. The manufacturer dealer relationships Fieldwork is establishing before the first tower goes up are the same relationships that make the second and third node cheaper and faster to build. The USDA grant application becomes the template for the next application. The LLC structure, the fire department anchor model, the five-stream revenue design — all of it transfers.

When Fieldwork builds Salmon Creek, it builds the instruction set for building every node after it.

What makes the corridor work

The MMBI fiber backbone running parallel to US 101 doesn't end at Garberville. It runs north through Leggett toward Willits, and south through the communities Fieldwork is already in conversation with. Every community in that corridor has the same ingredients: ridgeline access, sun exposure, fire departments with the same unmet need, and a population that has never been served by commercial broadband at a price that makes sense.

Every node in that corridor also runs ATAK. Every fire department on the 101 corridor gets shared situational awareness the day their node goes up — the same capability that costs almost nothing to include and that no corporate tower has ever offered. That is what replication means here: not just broadband delivered again, but the entire model deployed again — including the parts that don't have a billing line for the fire department.

The corridor doesn't require Fieldwork to solve a new problem at each site. It requires Fieldwork to execute the same solution in a new location.
